Aretha Franklin (1942 - 2018)

Queen of Soul
Aretha Louise Franklin, an American singer, songwriter, and pianist.

    Time Line of Aretha Franklin's Life

  • 1942 - Born on the 25th of March in Memphis, Tennessee
  • 1952 - Her mother died of a heart attack. During this time, she learned how to play the piano by ear.
  • 1956 - She released her first single, "Never Grow Old", backed with "You Grow Closer".
  • 1958 - She went on a tour with Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. at the age of 16. In 1968, she sang at his funeral.
  • 1960 - Released her first Columbia single, "Today I Sing the Blues". It reached the top ten of the Hot Rhythm & Blues Sellers chart.
  • 1961 - Columbia issued her first secular album, "Aretha: With The Ray Bryant Combo".
  • 1962 - Columbia issued two more albums and her album titled "The Tender, the Moving, the Swinging Aretha Franklin" reached No.69 on the Billboard Chart.
  • 1965 - Her ballad song, "Runnin' Out of Fools" reached the top ten on the R&B chart.
  • 1966 - She did not renew her contract with Columbia and signed a contract to Atlantic.
  • 1967 - Her song, "I Never Loved a Man (The Way I Love You)" reached number one on the R&B chart, and also peaked at number 9 on the Billboard 100. This is her first top-ten pop single.

    Her song "Respect" shot to number one on both the R&B and pop charts. It became her signature song and was later praised as a civil rights and feminist anthem.

  • 1980 - The Arista Era. After leaving Atlantic records, Aretha signed with Clive Davis's Arista Records. In that same year, she gave a performance at London's Royal Albert Hall in front of Queen Elizabeth.
  • 1985 - Her album called "Who's Zoomin' Who?" became her first Arista album to be certified platinum.
  • 2015 - She gave an acclaimed performance at the Kennedy Center Honors for honoree Carol King.
  • 2017 - She released the album "A Brand New Me" with the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra.
  • 2018 - She died at her home due to her illness. She was surrounded by her friends and family.
